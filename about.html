About Me

I am a Machine Learning Research Scientist, leading the machine learning team of Micron Technology Inc. as part of its Advanced Computing and Solution (ACS) group. Most of my research hours are invested in developing and training CNN/RNN architectures in supervised, unsupervised, as well as semi-supervised way. Applied research intrigues me, so I not only focus on getting better accuracy but also on getting better real-time performance on embedded devices as well as GPUs.

I completed my PhD from Purdue University in 2019 under Prof. Eugenio Culurciello as part of e-Lab. During my PhD I was also one of the founding members of our successful start up FwdNxt where I led its machine learning team. I received my undergraduate degree from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati, India and my Masters degree from Hanyang University, South Korea in 2012 and 2014 respectively.

I am mostly interested in developing deep neural network architectures which not only stand out in terms of accuracy, but can also be used in real life applications. Scene understanding is an important part of any machine learning task and I am working on multi-task learning for self-driving vehicles, augmented reality, etc. This serves as a major motivation for me to develop networks which can not only run on GPUs but also on embedded devices such as NVIDIA Tegra and Micron Deep Learning Accelerator (MDLA). I have also been spending significant amount of time working on developing unsupervised techniques for image enhancement and segmentation of medical images.

My google scholar profile can be found here. To look into some of the codes written by me, go to my Github page.
